Bao

Bao is a board game from Africa, with many different variations. You can play it online here. A description of the rules can be found in the first link, as well as here. As Bao is complicated, we will consider a simpler version, Kalah, for the final session, we will discuss Bao and Bao la kujifunza.

Kalah

We will consider the rules given here, where we have a board with 12 holes and 2 stores. Initially, there are 4 seeds in each hole. The game has two players who take turns to play. A turn for a player, consists of choosing one of the holes on their side of the board, picking all the seeds and then placing them in subsequent holes, one by one in an anti-clockwise direction.

          Mchezaji A
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | 4 | 4 | 4 | 4 | 4 | 4 | B |
| 0 |---|---|---|---|---|---| 0 |
|   | 4 | 4 | 4 | 4 | 4 | 4 |   |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
          Mchezaji B

Rules

  • A turn for a player, consists of choosing one of the holes on their side of the board, picking all the seeds and then placing them in subsequent holes, one by one in an anti-clockwise direction.
  • Players store is included in sowing, but opponents store is not.
  • If last grain ends up in the players store, player gets to play again.
  • If the last grain ends up in an empty hole on the players side, the player takes all the grains in the opposite hole on the opponents side, and the players last grain, and puts it in their store. The turn ends.
  • If the last grain is put anywhere else, the turn ends.
  • The game ends when one player has no grains on their side.
  • The winner is the player with the most grains in their store at the end of the game.
