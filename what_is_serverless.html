What is serverless?

Typical cloud computing has required setup of a computer that is always on and accessible through the internet. For e-commerce, such a computer will typically run the e-commerce website, payment solution gateway and a database to keep track of inventory.

In many cases, the compute resources are not always fully utilized or it may be the case that sales peak during a holiday season or special event, and so extra computing resources are required. Serverless computing allows for the provisioning of compute resources on demand, thus minimizing costs for compute resources if these are not fully utilized and allowing quick expansion of compute resources should it be required.

Here, we will examine a very low cost version of serverless computing that will deploy a static website with a shopping cart. Static websites have gained in popularity because they can be put on managed servers close to the end consumer, enabling a fast response time and short content downloading time.
